Package and present

A course by OpenUp

Course summary

  • Understand the basics of user experience design and wireframing for visualisation
  • Apply fundamental design principles to the production of data visualisations
  • Package and present your data to tell a compelling narrative
  • Course length: two days

Interested? Email us

 

Visualising data is one of the final ways in which we package data for communication. To do this effectively we need a clear understanding of which portion of our data tells or enriches our story.

We also need to understand what our audience requires in order to interpret or access the information represented by our visualisation and how to translate that into our design through our use of colour, type and composition.

This course presents a comprehensive introduction to visualising our data for the purposes of storytelling by focusing on the creation of, and the integration of, data visualisations in the form of interactive charts and graphs, infographics and maps suitable for use in offline and online environments.

Objectives

  • Establish a set of principles and guidelines to consider when visualising your data
  • Distinguish the role of function and purpose in the production of a data visualisation
  • Identify which data visualisation tools to use in which circumstance
  • Demonstrate an awareness of mobile-friendly and responsive design
  • Guidelines to compiling the narrative that delivers insights aimed at generating impact, in the format best suited to the stakejolder environment
  • Best practise for utilising social media to increase your network, and get your stories heard

Modules

  • Visual Encoding
  • From user journey to wireframe
  • Designing your visual narrative
  • Context-relevant packaging and presentation
  • Online Mapping
