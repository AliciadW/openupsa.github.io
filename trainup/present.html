Visualising data is one of the final ways in which we package data for communication. To do this effectively we need a clear understanding of which portion of our data enriches our story. We also need to understand what our audience requires in order to interpret the information that we are visualising and how best to translate that into our design through our use of colour, type and composition. This course weaves the element of data visualisation into the storytelling process, and explores the many forms in which the data could be represented throughout our narrative in order to ensure effective delivery of our message.

Objectives

  • Establish a set of principles and guidelines to consider when visualising your data
  • Distinguish the role of function and purpose in the production of a data visualisation
  • Identify which data visualisation tools to use in which circumstance
  • Demonstrate an awareness of mobile-friendly and responsive design
  • Guidelines to compiling a narrative that delivers insights aimed at generating impact, in the format best suited to the stakeholder environment
  • Techniques for leveraging social media to get your stories heard
