Short Courses

OpenUp offers short courses in data-driven storytelling for newsrooms, full-time and freelance journalists, activists, and people working in non-profit and non-governmental organisations.

We also offer data-driven storytelling training for people working in the public and private sectors who need to communicate information in an effective and easy-to-understand way.

Find and clean data

This course presents a comprehensive introduction to the fundamentals of data journalism as a method for practicing storytelling with data.

It walks participants through what data is, who works with it, and what can be done with data. It also provides participants with a blueprint to follow in order to effectively process data to prepare it for storytelling, in the form of the data pipeline.

  • Introduction to what data is and the terminology associated with working with data
  • Equip yourself with the core concepts and terminology used in the field of data journalism so they are able to critically engage in discussions around the role of data in communication
  • Insight into the different roles, skills and workflow that make up a data unit

Frequently asked questions

At what venue are the open intakes facilitated?

Venue will be advised 1 week prior to training to all attendees.

What are the general course requirements?

  • Participants need access to their own laptop
  • Participants need their own activated gmail account
  • Participants should have access to the Google Chrome browser

Does OpenUp subsidise any training and/or travel?

A 10% discount is available for NPOs on all short courses. Travel subsidies are unfortunately not available.

How much do courses cost and what are the payment options?

Courses cost R2,300 per person per day. 50% deposit is required on application, the balance being due before training commences.

Do you have online courses available?

We are publishing [tutorials and content] on basic data literacy knowledge and skills as well as open software usage free.

When are your upcoming courses?

Please check our event calendar here

Are your courses only around data?

Our courses are shaped around data processing for storytelling, ie for anyone who needs to work with data in order to communicate insights to stakeholders.

Please may we have some more information about your courses?

Our courses run in one of 2two ways - either internally for organisations who have a large enough group for us to facilitate internal training, or alternatively as scheduled open intakes based on a minimum number of participants.

Are your courses only available to organisations?

Our open intakes are open to the public based on scheduling.

What is the minimum number of students you take per course for group bookings?

Our courses run for a minimum of 8 and a maximum of 15 Learners.

How long are your courses?

Our courses run for between one & two days, depending on the required modules.

What is the cost of internal organisational training per course?

We prefer to compile modules for internal organisational based on a consultative process with organisations to ensure that we cover content suitable to the organisation's needs. We also provide the option to shape curriculum for internal training around an organisation's own data, to ensure the relevant skills transfer, which may entail a small additional cost.

Why isn’t your training free?

OpenUp’s Data Literacy Programme consists of 2 main endeavours:

Firstly, through our Codebridge grant we fund free data literacy training for identified segments of the population i.e. youth, school children, or those who are unable to afford commercial training opportunities. (More content on this soon)

We are publishing tutorials and content on basic data literacy knowledge and skills, as well as open software usage, free. Additionally our material is shared with the School of Data, with whom we collaborate on curriculum development.

Secondly, Short Courses are facilitated at commercial rates as a profit stream that contributes to the sustainability of our organisation, and in support of some of our other projects. Our short course curriculum will equip Learners with the skills our team has acquired through the practise and teaching of this material, as it relates to the Data Processing Pipeline.

Why Data Literacy?

OpenUp, and indeed the entire international Open Movement, has been aiming to, and will continue to liberate as much useful data as we can.

But are people able to use this data?

Data literacy & data processing skills are critical to providing people with access to any data. At OpenUp our theory of change is ‘If people are given information about their situation, how change can be achieved and the means to do so, they will often take action to improve their lives”. Part of providing information, is providing solutions to data literacy challenges. Thus are people enabled to make informed decisions.

Why Storytelling?

We explore these decisions through the lens of storytelling. Through the ages storytelling has been used to impart information. The model of data-driven journalism provides the ethics, the framework, and the verification procedures necessary to do this authentically.
