Frontend Developer (Intermediate)

We’re looking for an intermediate frontend developer to help us build useful, intuitive, open-source websites and tools.

Our projects range from small, single-page embeddable tools to large websites with rich content. The succesful applicant will help design and implement user-focused projects, with a particular emphasis on understanding and supporting user needs. This is an opportunity to dig deep to ensure users get the most out of what we build.

Candidates should be well-versed in modern frontend development tools and best-practices, be strong advocates for a good user experience, and be able to work well independently and collaboratively.

We're particularly interested in candidates that share our passion for working to help South Africans make informed decisions.

The position is full-time and based in Cape Town.

Requirements

  • At least 3 years' experience building web applications and frontends
  • Solid knowledge of Javascript, jQuery, CSS3 and HTML5
  • Solid knowledge of at least some of the modern frontend technologies, such as Angular, Backbone, React, Handlebars, Underscore, D3, Vue, etc.
  • Experience implementing frontends using a REST API
  • Experience using agile processes in a small team
  • Familiarity with GitHub and GitHub Pages
  • Has their own laptop
  • Non-South Africans must have a valid work permit

How to apply: Apply for this position by sending an email to jobs@openup.org.za. Please attach both your CV and a cover letter.

Community Researcher and Coordinator

OpenUp, in partnership with Cape Town TV, is producing a TV series called Democracy in Action. The show explores how our democracy can be strengthened and citizens can become empowered to solve their own service delivery issues when they are informed and supported in their actions.

We’re looking for a Community Researcher and Coordinator to work with us to identify people and communities experiencing issues, for the show. The researcher will unpack social issues and develop proposed guides and solutions based on legislation, policy, best practises and citizen-centred design. This role will be required to work closely with the OpenUp team.

This role has the following focus areas:

  1. Identify and recruit people and communities experiencing issues and document the individual’s or community’s story - this might be in person or using online technologies to drive recruitment campaigns
  2. Identify a partner organisation working in the problem space and recruit the organisation as an domain area partner
  3. Manage partner relationships ensuring a healthy, productive collaboration environment
  4. Develop a workplan to manage their own activities, tasks, schedule and deliverables
  5. Perform requirements elicitation and analysis of issues identified
  6. Develop and validate solutions based on identified requirements
  7. Identify organisations to partner with to deliver the envisaged solution
  8. Work closely with users ensuring the solution has the intended outcome

The Community Researcher and Coordinator is expected to being able to work both independently and collaboratively and meet required targets.

We're particularly interested in candidates that share our passion for working to help South Africans make informed decisions.

The position is part-time and is a limited term contract, initially (3 months) based in Cape Town.

Requirements

  • At least 3 years experience running projects, preferably in the non-profit sector
  • Proven track record of delivery
  • Strong facilitation skills
  • Ability to manage and facilitate prioritisation decisions
  • Excellent communication skills, across all stakeholder levels
  • Ability to organise and synthesize large volumes of information provided by stakeholders
  • A strong understanding of the community space, preferably with established networks they could lean on
  • Comfortable using online collaboration tools, and Google Apps products
  • Strong attention to detail
  • Ability to self-manage
  • Ability to work independently as well as collaboratively
  • Experience in working in an agile, iterative environment is a bonus
  • Has their own laptop
  • Non-South Africans must have a valid work permit

How to apply: Apply for this position by sending an email to jobs@openup.org.za. Please attach both your CV and a cover letter.
